### Sprint 1 Summary
    Sprint Goal: Enable employees to successfully book and manage conference rooms without conflicts.
    Sprint Duration: 2 weeks 
    Team: Scrum Master, Product Owner, Developers
    Date: May 15, 2026

 ### Executive Summary
    Sprint 1 delivered the core room booking and capacity filtering functionality, giving employees the ability to search for and reserve conference rooms for the first time. Booking cancellation (Story #4) was not completed due to its dependency on Story #1 finishing late in the sprint, leaving insufficient time for implementation. The sprint goal was partially met — the foundation is stable and Story #4 carries over into Sprint 2 with its dependency now fully resolved.

### What was delivered
### Stories Done
    -Story#1: Basic Room Booking
    -Story#3: Room Capacity Filtering

### Value Delivered
    -Story#1: Employees can search available rooms by date and time, confirm a booking, and receive email and in-app confirmation. 
    -Story#3: Employees can enter an attendee count and see only rooms that meet the minimum capacity requirement. 

### What Did Not Complete (And Why)
    - Story#4: Booking Cancellation, Story #1 was only confirmed code-complete mid-sprint. By the time Story #4 could begin, there was insufficient sprint time remaining to implement.

### Key Blockers and Constraints
    -Impacts:  Story #4 had less than 3 days of available development time remaining when it could finally start
    -Standups: The dependency risk was visible in standups from day 6 onward but was not escalated as a formal blocker until day 9, it was too late to replan.
    -Mid-sprint checkpoint: A mid-sprint check on day 7 identified that Story #1 was at 70% completion. The team should have flagged Story #4 as at-risk at that point.
